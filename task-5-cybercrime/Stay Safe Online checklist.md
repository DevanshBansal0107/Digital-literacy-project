Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

In today’s digital world, students actively use online platforms for learning, communication, and financial transactions. Following proper safety practices helps in preventing cybercrime and protecting personal and financial information.

# General Online Safety Tips

*   Use strong and unique passwords for all accounts
*   Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible
*   Avoid clicking on suspicious or unknown links
*   Do not share personal information on public platforms
*   Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches
*   Be cautious while using public Wi-Fi networks
*   Download apps only from trusted and official sources
*   Think carefully before posting anything online

# Financial & UPI Safety Tips

*   Never share OTP, UPI PIN, or banking details with anyone
*   Do not scan QR codes to receive money
*   Always verify payment requests before approving
*   Avoid unknown payment links and fake customer care numbers

# Social Media Safety Tips

*   Keep your social media profiles private or limited
*   Avoid sharing location, phone number, or personal details publicly
*   Do not accept friend requests from unknown people
*   Be respectful and avoid posting harmful or offensive content
*   Report and block suspicious or abusive accounts

# Email Safety Tips

*   Do not open emails from unknown senders
*   Avoid clicking on suspicious email attachments or links
*   Check sender email addresses carefully
*   Use professional language in emails
*   Report phishing emails immediately

# Reporting Cybercrime

National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in

Helpline Number: 1930

# Conclusion

Awareness and responsible behaviour are key to staying safe online. By following these preventive measures, students can confidently use digital platforms while minimizing risks.
